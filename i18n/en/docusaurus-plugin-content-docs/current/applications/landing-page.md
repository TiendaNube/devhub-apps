# Landing Page

The installation of the application represents only the first step in the partner's journey. For those who will enter a managed and assisted onboarding flow, displaying a **Landing Page** provides greater **clarity, alignment, and understanding of the next steps** for the merchant.

## Essential Functions of the Landing Page

### 1. Clarity and Direction for the Next Step

- After installation, the Landing Page should act as a **visual guide**, directing the merchant on the next actions.
- This direction is essential to prevent a drop in engagement immediately after installing the application.

### 2. Preparation for Assisted Onboarding

- In flows that require management and support, the Landing Page should be the central point for the partner to:
  - complete the **pre-registration**;
  - schedule the first contact (when applicable); or
  - receive information that the responsible team will get in touch.

### 3. Resource Centralization

- The Landing Page works as a **temporary hub**, centralizing essential documentation such as **FAQs, tutorial videos, and partnership policies**.
- This optimizes the connection with the merchant, allowing them to obtain, in a **self-service** way, the initial knowledge needed about processes and next steps.

Without an intermediate guidance step, we risk merchants abandoning the application. Below, we compare two post-installation flow scenarios:

### Scenario 1: Direct Installation (without landing page)

In this model, the merchant installs the app and is immediately confronted with the Login/Authentication screen.

- **The Problem:** If the merchant discovered the app through the App Store and does not yet have a commercial partnership or active account, they will encounter an impassable barrier.
- **The Frustration:** How do they obtain credentials?
  - Is there a registration portal?
  - Where are the support or sales channels?
- **Consequence:** Without clear instructions, the merchant uninstalls the application for not knowing how to proceed with the partnership, resulting in a lost business opportunity.
- **The Experience Failure (UX):** In many implementations, the adopted solution is direct redirection to the institutional website. This practice is not considered a good experience because:
  - **Context Break:** The user is abruptly removed from the native application environment to an external browser, causing disorientation.
  - **Conversion Friction:** The effort required to leave the app, fill out forms on another site, and then return to the app drastically increases the abandonment rate.
  - **Perception of Value:** The app stops appearing as a working tool and starts being seen only as a "shortcut" to a website, reducing the relevance of the installation.

### Scenario 2: Optimized Flow (with landing page)

By including a Landing Page right after installation, we provide a clear map for two distinct user profiles:

1. For users who are already partners, the landing page offers a direct and fast path.
   - **Action:** "I am already a partner / Log in" button.
   - **Result:** Smooth and frictionless experience for those who already know what to do.
2. For prospects (new users), the landing page acts as an onboarding guide.
   - **Action:** "I want to become a partner" or "Learn more" button.
   - **Onboarding Flow:**
     - **Visibility:** Quick presentation of the app's benefits.
     - **Next Steps:** Clear instructions on how to start the commercial partnership.
     - **Conversion:** Direction to a contact form, sales WhatsApp, or pre-registration page.

     ![Onboarding](../../../../../static/img/en/onboarding.png "Onboarding")

## Benefits of the Flow

- **Increased activation rate:** Well-informed and properly guided partners are more likely to complete onboarding and become active users of the application.
- **Early qualification:** The Landing Page can include forms that help qualify and segment the partner, allowing a more personalized approach before the first contact.
- **Professional and trustworthy experience:** A structured flow conveys professionalism and trust, reinforcing the value of the partnership and the application from the beginning.
